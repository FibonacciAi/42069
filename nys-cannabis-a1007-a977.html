NY Cannabis Legislation: Bills A1007 & A977

The War on Marijuana Continues: Overregulation in a Legal State

Assembly Bill A1007

This bill proposes banning smoking or vaping cannabis within 30 feet of children or any location where they reside, learn, or play. While it claims to protect children from secondhand cannabis smoke, in practice it criminalizes cannabis users in densely populated areas and urban settings, where legal consumption becomes impossible.

  • Thinly Veiled Goal: Lawmakers continue to stigmatize cannabis use, treating it as more dangerous than tobacco or alcohol.
  • Problem: Urban renters with no private outdoor spaces are effectively banned from legally consuming a product New York says is legal.
  • Deceptive Framing: Masked as "child protection," this bill ignores that parents already have discretion over cannabis use in their households.

Assembly Bill A977

This bill caps THC potency to 15% for cannabis flower and 25% for other cannabis products, claiming to address adverse health effects. But the reality is that this restriction would cripple the legal cannabis market while driving consumers to the unregulated black market.

  • Disastrous Consequences: Lower THC limits would make New York dispensaries uncompetitive, leaving consumers seeking higher-THC products elsewhere.
  • Health Risks: Pushing users to untested and unregulated black-market products creates more harm than good.
  • Hypocrisy: Alcohol strength isn't capped, yet cannabis continues to face unfair scrutiny despite being legalized.

Why This Matters

  • High Impact: These bills attempt to re-criminalize marijuana use, disproportionately targeting renters, urban residents, and low-income communities.
  • Medium Impact: Restrictive potency caps undermine New York's cannabis industry, threatening jobs and tax revenue.
  • Low Impact: Proponents claim these measures are about health and safety, but the actual benefits are minimal compared to the harm of driving users to unregulated products.

Legalizing cannabis was supposed to end the criminalization of users and create a safe, regulated market. Overregulation like this undoes that progress and reinforces the stigma against cannabis. Hands off our weed!
