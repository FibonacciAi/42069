3-Year Cycles, 3.33 & I-Ching Connections

1. Why Not 3? Or 3.33?

The focus on 4-year cycles can overshadow shorter or fractional cycles that also influence markets. Let’s explore 3‑year and 3.33‑year intervals:

1.1. 3-Year Cycles

  • Historical Market Connections: Stock markets have shown 3-year bullish or bearish phases where conditions reset after sharp corrections.
  • Hedge Fund Swaps: Some derivatives (credit default swaps, interest rate swaps) often roll over every 3 years, hinting 2023–2026 could be a crucial window.
  • Cultural Cycles: Certain traditions (e.g., in Chinese culture) treat 3 years as a distinct transformative period.

1.2. 3.33 Years

  • Mathematical Approximation: 3.33 is roughly one-third of a 10-year macro cycle. Some cyclical phenomena aren’t precise integers, so fractional durations (e.g., 3.33) may emerge.
  • Numerology & Physics: 3.33 can resonate with Fibonacci-like growth patterns. Certain algorithmic trading strategies exploit these “thirds,” tying into fractal/ Mandelbrot concepts.
