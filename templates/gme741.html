The Core Conflict

The GME saga represents a seminal clash between institutional finance and a new, powerful force of decentralized retail investors, exposing deep fissures in the modern market.

The "Mother of All Short Squeezes" (MOASS) Thesis

The MOASS theory refers to a hypothetical market event where GME's price would skyrocket to unprecedented levels. This is predicated on the belief that various financial institutions held a massive short position, at times reportedly exceeding 100% of the total outstanding shares. The core idea is that a significant portion of GME's shares in circulation are "synthetic" or "phantom" shares created through practices like naked shorting. These represent a massive hidden liability that must eventually be covered by purchasing real shares. When forced to cover, these entities would have to buy shares at any price, leading to an exponential price increase, intensified by the relatively small number of legitimate GME shares and the removal of shares from the market via Direct Registration (DRS).
