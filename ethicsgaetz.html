House Ethics Committee Report Analysis: Rep. Matt Gaetz

On December 23, 2024, the House Ethics Committee released its report detailing findings from an investigation into allegations against former Representative Matt Gaetz. The investigation found substantial evidence that Gaetz engaged in multiple violations of state and federal laws, House rules, and standards of conduct between 2017-2020.

Sexual Misconduct

• Found substantial evidence that Gaetz paid women for sexual activity from 2017-2020
• Determined Gaetz engaged in sexual activity with a 17-year-old girl in July 2017
• Documented tens of thousands of dollars in payments to multiple women through various payment platforms
• Did not find sufficient evidence to conclude Gaetz violated federal sex trafficking laws

Drug Use

• Found substantial evidence of cocaine, ecstasy, and marijuana use
• Multiple witnesses testified to observing Gaetz using illegal substances
• Evidence showed Gaetz used a pseudonymous email account to purchase marijuana

Gift Rule Violations

• Accepted impermissible gifts including private plane travel and lodging during a 2018 Bahamas trip
• Failed to seek required Committee approval for gifts exceeding value thresholds
• Did not properly disclose gifts on Financial Disclosure forms

Misuse of Official Resources

• Used congressional staff to assist a woman with whom he had a sexual relationship in obtaining an expedited passport
• Woman was not a constituent of his district

Obstruction

• Failed to comply with Committee subpoenas
• Provided minimal documentation in response to requests
• Declined voluntary interview and failed to appear for compelled testimony
• Made misleading statements about cooperation with investigation
• Investigation began April 9, 2021
• Initially deferred at DOJ's request until February 2023
• Gaetz resigned from House on November 14, 2024
• Committee issued report despite losing jurisdiction after resignation
• Several Committee members dissented on decision to release report after resignation
• Reviewed nearly 14,000 documents
• Contacted over two dozen witnesses
• Issued 29 subpoenas
• Made multiple attempts to obtain Gaetz's testimony
• Faced significant delays due to DOJ's limited cooperation

The report represents a rare instance of the Ethics Committee releasing findings after a member's resignation. The Committee majority determined public release was warranted given the serious nature of the findings, though several members dissented on jurisdictional grounds.
